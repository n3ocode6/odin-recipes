Appam (Kerala-Style Pancakes)

Ingredients

  • 1 cup raw rice
  • 1/4 cup cooked rice (preferably leftover rice)
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp active dry yeast
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 to 3/4 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • 2 tbsp coconut milk (optional, for extra softness)

Instructions

  1. Soak the rice: Wash the raw rice thoroughly and soak it in water for about 4-6 hours, or overnight for best results.
  2. Prepare the batter: After soaking, drain the rice and grind it along with cooked rice and grated coconut into a smooth batter. Add a little water if necessary to help the grinding process.
  3. Activate the yeast: In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in a little warm water. Let it sit for about 10 minutes, until it becomes frothy.
  4. Combine everything: Add the activated yeast mixture and salt to the rice-coconut batter and mix well. The batter should have a pourable consistency but not too runny. Add more water if needed.
  5. Ferment the batter: Cover the batter and allow it to ferment for 8-12 hours, or overnight, in a warm place. The batter should double in size and have a slightly sour smell, which is a sign of proper fermentation.
  6. Cook the Appams: Heat an appam pan or a non-stick pan over medium heat. Lightly grease the pan with oil or butter. Pour a ladleful of batter into the center of the pan, then tilt and swirl the pan to spread the batter into a thin layer around the edges, leaving the center thicker.
  7. Cover and cook: Cover the pan with a lid and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the edges become crispy and golden, and the center is cooked through. The appam should be soft in the middle and slightly crisp on the outside.
  8. Serve: Serve the appam hot with stew, curry, or coconut milk. Appams are best enjoyed fresh and hot!
